## What is covered

VaultWares' limited hardware warranty covers:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Defects in materials and workmanship">
    Manufacturing defects, component failures under normal use conditions, and premature failure of electrical components are covered under the standard warranty.

    **Examples**:
    - USB connector failure under normal use
    - Firmware update failure resulting in a bricked device
    - Premature failure of the fingerprint sensor
    - Power supply failure in VaultHSM appliances
    - Persistent LED malfunction
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="DOA (Dead on Arrival)">
    If a product is non-functional immediately upon receipt, VaultWares will ship a replacement within 2 business days upon verification. Contact [support@vaultwares.com](mailto:support@vaultwares.com) within 30 days of delivery.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Functional defects">
    If a device fails to perform its advertised functions (encryption, authentication, cryptographic operations) as specified in the product datasheet, it is covered under warranty.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What is NOT covered

The following are explicitly excluded from warranty coverage:

- **Physical damage**: Drops, impacts, crushing, liquid immersion (except VaultDrive Rugged within IP68 rating)
- **Intentional destruction**: Triggered crypto-erase or self-destruct features are not covered — these are working as designed
- **Unauthorized modifications**: Opening the tamper-evident enclosure voids the warranty
- **Normal wear and tear**: Cosmetic scratches, worn USB connectors after years of normal use
- **Lost or stolen devices**: Warranty is tied to the device, not the owner
- **Data recovery**: VaultWares cannot recover encrypted data under any circumstances — this is by design
- **Third-party damage**: Damage caused by third-party equipment, power surges, or environmental factors
- **Misuse**: Use outside the specified operating conditions (temperature, humidity, voltage)

<Warning>
  VaultWares tamper-detection features will trigger automatic key destruction and may render the device non-functional if the enclosure is opened. This is a security feature, not a defect, and is not covered under warranty.
</Warning>

## How to make a warranty claim

<Steps>
  <Step title="Verify warranty status">
    Check your product's warranty status at [portal.vaultwares.com/warranty](https://portal.vaultwares.com/warranty) using the device serial number.

    Alternatively:
    ```bash
    # Check warranty status via CLI
    vw-admin warranty-status --serial VD-ENT-00247
    # Output:
    # Serial: VD-ENT-00247
    # Product: VaultDrive Enterprise
    # Purchase date: 2021-11-15
    # Warranty expires: 2028-11-15
    # Status: Active
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Contact support">
    Email [support@vaultwares.com](mailto:support@vaultwares.com) or open a ticket at [portal.vaultwares.com/support](https://portal.vaultwares.com/support). Include:
    - Device serial number
    - Proof of purchase (order number or invoice)
    - Detailed description of the failure
    - Steps to reproduce (if applicable)
    - Photos or videos demonstrating the defect
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive RMA authorization">
    If the warranty claim is approved, you will receive an RMA (Return Merchandise Authorization) number within 2 business days. See the [RMA process](/support/rma) for detailed return instructions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ship the defective unit">
    Package and ship the defective unit as instructed. VaultWares covers return shipping for warranty claims.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Receive replacement or repaired unit">
    Depending on the warranty service level, you will receive either a replacement unit or the repaired original. Firmware and configuration are not guaranteed to be restored — ensure you have backups.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Frequently asked questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Does warranty transfer if I sell or transfer the device?">
    Warranty coverage is tied to the original purchaser's organization. Transfer of warranty requires a formal transfer request submitted to [support@vaultwares.com](mailto:support@vaultwares.com) with documentation of the transfer.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I recover data from a warranty replacement?">
    No. Because of hardware encryption, data on a failed device cannot be recovered — even by VaultWares. Ensure you have current backups of all important data. Replacement devices ship factory-fresh.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="What happens to my encryption keys if I return a device?">
    Before returning any device, initiate a crypto-erase to destroy all key material. If the device cannot be powered on, VaultWares will perform a physical destruction procedure and provide a certificate of destruction on request.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
